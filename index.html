Practical Lean Foundations

A guided series introducing Lean thinking, continuous improvement, and the core principles and behaviors behind effective, sustainable process excellence. Each module includes a short summary and the video link.

How to use this material

These videos build on each other. Start with the course introduction, move into Lean as a mindset, then learn how improvement actually happens through the PDSA cycle. From there, explore cultural barriers that get in the way and finish with the five core Lean principles, sources of waste, and how to measure and sustain improvement.

Tip: Treat Lean not as a set of tools, but as a way of seeing work, respecting the people who do the work, and improving a little bit every day.

1 Introduction and Course Objectives

Video: https://youtu.be/8xmoKR_pWs0

Teresa Heyman introduces the Practical Lean Course, a 12-module program focused on continuous improvement and Lean methodology. Learners will understand Lean as a mindset, distinguish value-added from waste, and use the Plan-Do-Study-Act (PDSA) cycle for improvement.

2 Lean Philosophy and Core Concepts

Video: https://youtu.be/wHTXjWUdJT8

Lean is a philosophy of continuous improvement and respect for people. It emphasizes empowering those who perform the work to improve it and focuses on delivering value as defined by the customer while reducing waste in every process.

3 Scientific Method and PDSA Cycle

Video: https://youtu.be/HltQc6H5Pho

Lean uses the Plan-Do-Study-Act (PDSA) cycle to test ideas, learn from results, and standardize improvements. Continuous small experiments generate lasting change that outperforms large, one-time overhauls.

4 Common Barriers and Cultural Aspects

Video: https://youtu.be/9v5wrLFMUS4

Common Lean barriers include “Not Invented Here” resistance and the idea that teams are too busy to improve. Overcoming these requires a culture of respect, openness, and embedding improvement into daily work.

5 Five Principles of Lean

Video: https://youtu.be/BQomAD8pepw

This module introduces Lean’s five principles: define value, map the value stream, create flow, establish pull, and pursue perfection — a continuous loop for operational excellence.

6 Four Goals of Lean and The Eight Wastes

Video: https://youtu.be/uniBpCnRsTQ

The four goals of Lean are to improve quality, free up resources, reduce lead time, and reduce total costs. The eight wastes — transport, inventory, motion, waiting, overproduction, over-processing, defects, and unutilized talent — are key targets for elimination.

7 Metrics for Lean Improvement

Video: https://youtu.be/ZQKZWPCZAzo

Lean improvement depends on measuring progress in categories like Safety, Quality, Cost, Delivery, and Morale. Meaningful metrics help track real improvements and sustain gains over time.

8 Summary and Continuous Improvement Guidelines

Video: https://youtu.be/arY8-PAvToI

Lean is a continuous journey. This closing module revisits Lean principles, reinforcing the idea that improvement, respect for people, and learning are everyday practices — not one-time initiatives.
